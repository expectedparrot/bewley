Interviewer: Thanks for joining us today, Bob. How long have you been
using the platform?

Bob: About six months. I came in through a colleague's recommendation.

Interviewer: What was your first impression?

Bob: Mixed. The interface looked clean but I couldn't find basic settings.
I spent the first week just trying to configure notifications. That was
frustrating.

Interviewer: Did you consider switching to something else?

Bob: I did, actually. There was a competitor product that seemed simpler.
But my colleague convinced me to stick with it, saying the advanced
features were worth the learning curve.

Interviewer: And was that true?

Bob: Partially. The analytics dashboard is genuinely excellent. I use it
every day and it's saved me hours of manual reporting. But the integration
with our existing tools was painful. We had to build custom middleware
just to get the API to play nicely with our stack.

Interviewer: How would you describe the support experience?

Bob: Support was hit or miss. When I had a billing issue they resolved it
in hours. But technical questions would sit in the queue for days. I ended
up relying on community forums more than official support.

Interviewer: Any other workarounds you've developed?

Bob: A few. We have a shared wiki page of tips and tricks that my team
maintains. Things like "always export in JSON, never CSV" and "restart
the sync agent every Monday." It works but it's not ideal.

Interviewer: Would you recommend it to others?

Bob: With caveats, yes. It's powerful but it's not plug and play. You need
someone technical on your team to get the most out of it.
