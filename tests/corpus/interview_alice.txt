Interviewer: Can you tell me about your experience with the platform?

Alice: Sure. I've been using it for about two years now. At first I was
really skeptical because I'd had bad experiences with similar tools before.
But after the first month I started to trust it more.

Interviewer: What changed your mind?

Alice: Honestly, it was the transparency. I could see exactly what was
happening with my data. That built a lot of trust over time. Even when
things went wrong — like the outage last March — they were upfront about
it. That meant a lot to me.

Interviewer: Were there any friction points?

Alice: Oh definitely. The onboarding was rough. The documentation was
scattered and I had to figure out a lot on my own. I ended up developing
my own workarounds for things that should have been straightforward.

Interviewer: Can you give me a specific example?

Alice: Yeah, the export feature. It was supposed to handle CSV but it kept
mangling Unicode characters. So I wrote a little script to clean up the
output every time. It worked, but it shouldn't have been necessary.

Interviewer: How do you feel about it now overall?

Alice: I trust it. I really do. But I think they could do better on the
onboarding side. The core product is solid once you get past that initial
learning curve.
