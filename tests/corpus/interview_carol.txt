Interviewer: Carol, thank you for speaking with us. What's your role?

Carol: I'm a project manager. I oversee a team of twelve and we use the
platform for all our client reporting.

Interviewer: How did you come to adopt it?

Carol: It was a top-down decision. Leadership chose it based on the
enterprise features — SSO, audit logging, that sort of thing. We didn't
have much say in the matter.

Interviewer: How did your team react?

Carol: Resistance, mostly. People were comfortable with the old system.
The transition was rocky. We lost about two weeks of productivity during
the switchover because the data migration tool didn't handle our custom
fields correctly.

Interviewer: That sounds difficult. How did you manage it?

Carol: A lot of manual work. I personally re-entered about 200 records
that got corrupted in the migration. It was not a good experience.

Interviewer: Has it improved since then?

Carol: Dramatically, actually. Once everything was set up properly, the
workflow improvements were significant. Our reporting time dropped by
about 40%. The automated dashboards alone justified the switch.

Interviewer: What about reliability?

Carol: Rock solid in my experience. We've had maybe one unplanned outage
in eight months. And their status page is honest — they don't try to
hide incidents, which I appreciate.

Interviewer: Any features you wish existed?

Carol: Better bulk operations. When I need to update 50 records at once,
I have to do them one by one or use the API. For a project manager who
doesn't code, that's a real limitation.

Interviewer: Would you choose it again if starting fresh?

Carol: Knowing what I know now, yes. But I'd budget more time for the
migration and I'd push for better training resources upfront.
